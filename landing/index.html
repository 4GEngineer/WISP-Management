WISP operations and bandwidth proof—two tools, one field story

Run the network. Prove the throughput.

WISP Management is where you run subscribers, sites, gear, and plans. Bandwidth Test Manager is where you schedule Ookla / iperf (and netperf) runs, keep history, and wire SLA-style alerts—so performance stays tied to how you operate.

Built from long-range RF and access work through full-stack ISP systems: wisptools.io keeps the story on operators—what you deploy, what you promise customers, and what you can prove on the wire.

How the two pieces fit

Same subscribers, same plant—different jobs, one workflow.

  • WISP Management holds customers, service locations, bandwidth plans, CPE via TR-069/ACS, and the rest of day-to-day operations (including optional Voice/SIP modules) so your team has a single place for “who has what.”
  • Bandwidth Test Manager runs independent probes and tests from a purpose-built UI: measure throughput and latency on the access path, keep results over time, and hook webhooks when things drift.
  • Together, you define and sell service in Management—then demonstrate it with BTM. NOC and field teams can line up test endpoints with the same sites and circuits you already model; when performance drops, you have both inventory context and measurement history.

Open source & acknowledgments

These are two MIT-licensed products (separate repos) for the same operators: fixed wireless, fiber, and hybrid ISPs. WISP Management builds on SvelteKit, Firebase, MongoDB, ECharts, Leaflet, and optional Esri ArcGIS; Bandwidth Test Manager focuses on probes, scheduling, and APIs. Hosted services (Firebase, Atlas, GCE, etc.) are your agreements.
